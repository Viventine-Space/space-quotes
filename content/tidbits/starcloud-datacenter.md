Starcloud, Inc. is asking the FCC for permission to put a data center in orbit. Its February 2,
2026 license application describes a constellation of satellites built to "operate as a
distributed datacenter to train and operate artificial intelligence ('AI') models and other
cloud computing services." The argument for doing this in space, rather than in a warehouse on
the ground, is about physics and power: the filing says the satellites would tap "near-constant
solar power, radiative cooling, and the ability to scale sizes and power levels not possible on
earth."

This is an early-stage license request, not a launch. Starcloud says the constellation would
lean on third-party networks to reach users on the ground, and would have to operate in close
coordination with others using the same orbits.

> "near-constant solar power, radiative cooling, and the ability to scale sizes and power levels not possible on earth"
> — Starcloud, FCC license application
