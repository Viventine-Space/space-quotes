On February 19, 2026, **Planet Labs PBC** filed reply comments in FCC docket 25-306 **strongly
supporting** the Commission's effort to "modernize and streamline its space and earth station
licensing regulations." Planet argues the "health and competitiveness of the U.S. satellite
industry depend on a regulatory environment that matches the speed of private-sector
innovation," and backs proposals including a nationwide immoveable earth station framework, a
shift "from a narrative-based to a certification-based licensing approach," and eliminating bond
requirements for NGSO systems with **fewer than 200 spacecraft**.

**Why it matters.** As operators fly more and more spacecraft, licensing rules built for a
handful of big satellites start to bite. Planet's filing is the high-volume operator's case for
rules that scale — lighter, certification-based, faster. It also draws a careful line on safety:
Planet supports new information-sharing certifications to "reduce orbital debris risk" but
rejects "unsupported, stringent new thresholds" beyond what the FCC or NASA propose. Docket
25-306 is busy — **121 filings**, including 107 comments.
