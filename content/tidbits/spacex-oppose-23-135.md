On April 23, 2025, **SpaceX** filed an opposition in FCC docket 23-135 urging the Commission to
**deny EchoStar's Application for Review**. EchoStar had asked the FCC to overturn a waiver its
Bureaus granted in connection with SpaceX's Supplemental Coverage from Space (SCS) operations —
the direct-to-cell service it's building with T-Mobile. SpaceX argues EchoStar's challenge
"ignores the very purpose of a waiver," and asks the Commission to reject it "to remove any
cloud over SpaceX's" authorization.

**Why it matters.** Direct-to-cell — beaming a signal from satellites straight to ordinary
phones — is one of the most valuable new frontiers in spectrum, and the rules are being written
in real time. Here a rival, EchoStar, is trying to unwind a regulatory decision that underpins
SpaceX's service, and SpaceX is fighting to keep it. The proceeding is contested: docket 23-135
holds **125 filings**, including 10 oppositions.

Neither, it turns out, is the FCC's waiver process — which is exactly what this fight is about.
