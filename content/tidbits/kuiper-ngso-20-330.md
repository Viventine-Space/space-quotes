On September 24, 2024, **Kuiper Systems LLC** — Amazon's satellite venture — filed an ex parte
letter urging the FCC to adopt its **Draft Report and Order** authorizing the **17.3–17.8 GHz
("17 GHz") band** for non-geostationary (NGSO) fixed-satellite service. Amazon argues that
commenters have built "a robust record" showing NGSO downlinks can share the band, and the
letter works to address concerns raised by **AT&T and Verizon** about coexistence with existing
Fixed Service operations.

**Why it matters.** Satellite broadband constellations are hungry for spectrum, and every new
band the FCC opens is contested ground between newcomers beaming from orbit and incumbents
already using the airwaves on the ground. Amazon is pushing to unlock more capacity for its
constellation while promising the existing users won't be harmed — the central bargain of
nearly every modern spectrum fight.

> "Space is for everybody. It's not just for a few people in science or math, or for a select group of astronauts."
> — Christa McAuliffe

McAuliffe's "everybody" now includes the spectrum to reach them. Opening a band like 17 GHz is
the unglamorous infrastructure behind the promise — the plumbing that decides whether satellite
broadband reaches the many or stays with the few.
