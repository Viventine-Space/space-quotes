On December 23, 2024, **Astroscale U.S. Inc.** filed an FCC satellite license application
(SAT-LOA-20241223-00298) for an unusual kind of spacecraft: not a communications satellite or
an imaging constellation, but an **in-space refueling demonstration vehicle**, described in the
application as a **geostationary space station**.

**Why it matters.** A refueling demonstration is an unusual thing to license: the vehicle's job
isn't to communicate or observe, but to show that refuelling can be done in space. And an FCC
authorization is one of the first concrete gates standing between that idea and an actual
mission — which is what makes a quiet license application worth noticing.

> "Earth is a small town with many neighborhoods in a very big universe."
> — Ron Garan

Every small town eventually needs more than new arrivals — it needs the services that keep it
running. A refueling demonstration is a quiet bet that the next phase of space is about the
infrastructure, not just the hardware.
