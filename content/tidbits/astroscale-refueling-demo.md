Astroscale wants to refuel satellites in orbit, and its FCC license application names the
spacecraft that would do it: APS-R, the Astroscale Prototype Servicer for Refueling. In the
December 23, 2024 filing, the company says APS-R "will be the first spacecraft to perform
cooperative in-space refueling demonstrations with client vehicles in supersynchronous
geostationary orbit."

The point is to prove the plumbing works. According to the application, the mission would
generate technical data on in-space fluid transport, the interfaces required to move fluid
between spacecraft, and spacecraft refueling capabilities.

> "the first spacecraft to perform cooperative in-space refueling demonstrations with client vehicles"
> — Astroscale, FCC license application
